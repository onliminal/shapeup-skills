# Framing: Lead Thread

## Problem

Home-service owner-operators lose pre-booking leads because customer context is split across email and SMS, and there is no lightweight queue showing who needs a follow-up next. A real example: a plumber replied to an estimate request by text, got photos by email, then forgot to respond when the customer followed up two days later.

## Who's Affected

Owner-operators at small home-service shops with fewer than ten employees who personally carry intake on a phone while also doing field work.

## Current Workaround

Personal iMessage, personal Gmail, inbox flags, a notes app, and memory.

## Why Now

More leads arrive through fragmented digital channels, response expectations are faster, and the founder now has enough direct signal that a focused wedge could be built without pretending to solve all small-business CRM.

## Appetite

1 week. This problem is worth a sharp first bet, not a broad platform build.

## Success Criteria

An owner-operator can see new and active leads in one place, open a combined thread, and leave the session knowing exactly who needs a follow-up next.

## Open Questions
- Should the v1 queue optimize for a solo operator only, or also support a spouse/office-manager helper?
- Is manual merge acceptable for duplicate leads in the first release?
- What is the most credible onboarding path for connecting SMS?

## Next Best Question
What is the simplest shaped concept that lets an operator review inbound leads, see unified context, and manage follow-up without dragging in CRM scope?
