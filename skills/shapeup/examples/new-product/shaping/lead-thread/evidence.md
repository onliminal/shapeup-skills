# Evidence: Lead Thread

## Wedge Under Test

Owner-operators at home-service businesses who personally manage inbound inquiries and lose follow-up because the thread is fragmented across email and SMS.

## Decision To Unlock

Does this wedge deserve framing as a specific small-batch product bet, or is the pain too weak or too broad?

## Key Assumptions
| Assumption | Importance | Uncertainty | Test |
|------------|------------|-------------|------|
| Missed-lead pain is frequent and costly | high | medium | Problem interviews |
| Operators will trust a unified thread built from real messages | high | high | Concierge test |
| Email + SMS is enough for a first wedge | medium | medium | Interviews plus concierge scope test |

## Experiment Plan

### Experiment 1: Recent-pain interviews
- Type: interview
- Audience: six owner-operators with fewer than ten employees
- Question: is missed follow-up a real, recent, repeated pain?
- Method: 30-minute calls focused on the last month, not hypothetical tooling
- Success Signal: four or more can tell a recent missed-lead story with a costly or embarrassing outcome
- Failure Signal: pain is vague, rare, or mostly solved with current tools
- Timebox: 4 days

### Experiment 2: Concierge unified thread
- Type: concierge
- Audience: two interviewees with active inbound leads
- Question: does seeing one combined thread plus a follow-up queue change behavior?
- Method: manually combine one day's email + SMS into a simple timeline and overdue list
- Success Signal: both participants say they would keep using the workflow and prefer it to current inbox juggling
- Failure Signal: they find it intrusive, unnecessary, or not meaningfully better than current habits
- Timebox: 2 days

## Evidence Collected

Five of six operators described a missed or delayed follow-up from the last month. Two linked it directly to lost revenue. Both concierge participants found the combined thread useful and immediately asked whether it could remind them who needed a reply tomorrow morning.

## Interpretation

The wedge is real enough to frame. The clearest signal was not "I want CRM," but "I lose the thread before the job gets booked." The pain is strongest for owner-operators still carrying intake themselves. Evidence also suggests the v1 wedge can begin with email + SMS and defer broader channel coverage.

## Recommendation

Move to Frame

## Open Questions
- Is manual follow-up state enough for v1, or is simple automation required to make the wedge feel complete?
- Does the best initial customer buy for solo operators or for two-person owner + office-manager teams?
- What is the simplest trustworthy SMS capture path?

## Next Best Question
What exact problem story should we frame so the bet stays about pre-booking follow-up chaos rather than drifting into general CRM scope?
