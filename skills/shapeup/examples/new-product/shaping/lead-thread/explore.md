# Explore: Lead Thread

## The Idea

A lightweight lead-management product for home-service owner-operators who lose inbound leads across text, email, voicemail, and web inquiries before those leads ever reach a formal CRM or job-management tool.

## The People

Owner-operators at small plumbing, HVAC, and electrical shops with 1-10 employees. They still run field jobs themselves, carry the sales conversation on a personal phone, and often have a spouse or part-time office manager helping inconsistently.

## Problem Space

Pre-booking lead flow is fragmented, easy to forget, and handled in tools that were not designed to preserve one customer thread.

### Problem 1: Missed follow-up

Estimate requests get an initial response, then disappear because the customer replies in a different channel or later than expected.

### Problem 2: Broken context

Photos, address details, and call notes live across email, text, and voicemail, so the operator can't see the whole conversation in one place.

### Problem 3: No active lead queue

There is no clear distinction between new leads, waiting leads, and overdue leads. The work lives in memory and inbox flags.

## Existing Landscape

Current workflow is a mix of iMessage, Gmail, a notes app, and memory. Jobber and Housecall Pro help after a job is booked, but they do not solve the earliest intake chaos. Shared inbox tools exist, but they are too generic and do not fit owner-operators using personal devices.

## Unique Angle

The founder has prior messaging-platform experience and direct access to 14 home-service operators through a local business group. The opportunity is not "small business CRM" in general, but the pre-booking lead thread that sits before formal scheduling software.

## Key Assumptions
| Assumption | Confidence | How to test |
|------------|------------|-------------|
| Missed-lead pain is frequent enough to pay for | medium | Problem interviews with recent lost-lead stories |
| Operators will connect their real channels if the workflow is simple | low | Concierge test using one day of real messages |
| The sharpest wedge is owner-operators, not office-managed shops | medium | Compare interviews across both groups |

## Candidate Wedges

### Wedge 1: Unified lead inbox for owner-operators

One thread showing pre-booking communication across email and SMS, plus a lightweight follow-up queue.

### Wedge 2: Follow-up reminder layer for quotes

Track who received a quote and has not replied, without trying to unify all channels.

### Wedge 3: Missed-call responder for field operators

Auto-capture voicemail or missed-call inquiries and convert them into a reply queue.

## Commitment Level

The founder is willing to spend two weeks on lightweight validation and a one-week wedge-specific build if the problem is real.

## Open Questions
- Will owner-operators connect personal phone/email accounts, or will that feel too invasive?
- Is the best first wedge solo operators, or shops with a part-time office manager?
- Can the product win with just email + SMS before adding voicemail or social channels?

## Next Best Question
What do real owner-operators say when asked for a recent missed-lead story, and will any of them try a concierge version using real messages?
